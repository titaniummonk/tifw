## 🕊️ Founder’s Mission Statement  
**By Wesley** (*a.k.a. ฉลาม – Chalaam*)

My name is Wesley. For the past ten years, I’ve called Thailand my home — not because I was born here, but because I chose to live in service of the people here.

Originally from the Netherlands, I was raised with a deep belief that helping others is one of the most meaningful things we can do. That belief led me to volunteer on ambulances with local rescue teams across Thailand. I worked without pay, driven only by purpose and compassion.

I’ve been living on **Samui Island** for over five years now, where I’ve built strong connections and a deep sense of belonging. Locally, I’ve been known by the nickname **“ฉลาม”** (*Chalaam*), meaning **“shark” in Thai** — a name that represents determination, presence, and always moving forward, even in the most challenging waters.

On **December 14th, 2023**, while guiding an ambulance through heavy rush-hour traffic with a stroke patient onboard, I was struck by a vehicle. The driver fled the scene, leaving me to face the consequences alone. What followed was a long and painful road — **months of immobility**, **severe nerve pain**, **loss of employment**, and **uncovered medical expenses totaling over 750,000 THB**.

<img src="/img/crash.jpg" alt="Accident aftermath – December 14th, 2023" style="max-width: 25%; max-height: 25%;" />

In february 2025, I underwent a **complex, first-of-its-kind 3D-printed titanium elbow replacement surgery** in Hat Yai, Thailand — a major milestone in my recovery. While the surgery was a success, the financial impact and rehabilitation process remain significant.

Out of this hardship, something bigger emerged.

The **Charoen Watthana Initiative** was born from my experience, but its purpose reaches far beyond it. This is **not yet a registered foundation** — it is a growing movement toward one. We are currently raising between **500,000 THB (for incorporation)** and **5–10 million THB** to fully establish the infrastructure, staffing, and programs of a sustainable nonprofit.

Our flagship vision is the **Mu Ko Samui Center**, the first of its kind, to be based on **Ko Samui** — funding dependent, on leased or owned land — and designed to serve the broader **Mu Ko Samui region**, including **Ko Pha-ngan** and **Ko Tao**. This center will operate 24/7, offering **digital and phone-based mental healthcare and general support services for all nationalities**, blending human compassion with AI support verified by professionals.

We also aim to:
- Guide and educate youth in Southern Thailand, beginning on Samui, using **technology and AI as tools for ethical learning** — grounded not in commercialism, but in **spiritual awareness, moral conduct, and mindful growth**
- Strengthen public health through realistic, inclusive, and locally grounded efforts
- Collaborate with other charitable organizations for greater public impact
- Maintain strict political neutrality

As founder, I am **fully committed** — not only in words, but in assets. I will personally **donate**:
- A **Mitsubishi Triton Athlete** and **Honda ADV150** for operations
- **51% of my shares** in existing Thai commercial companies  
  (allowing dividends to support the long-term financial health of the nonprofit)

This is not just a recovery story. It is a story of transformation — and the beginning of a greater mission to ensure that those who serve others never stand alone when they are the ones in need.

**Your support — in any form — can help bring this initiative to life.**

Thank you,  
**Wesley**  
(*a.k.a. ฉลาม – Chalaam*)
