## Partnership Overview

The Digital Nomad Investment Partnership, led by Wesley Steehouwer (The Titanium Monk), is a Scottish General Partnership formed under the Partnership Act 1890 and Limited Partnerships Act 1907. This agreement outlines the structure, terms, and objectives for a disciplined, long-term investment strategy targeting superior capital appreciation through undervalued securities, with a focus on Southeast Asian markets.

## Key Terms and Operations

**Management and Control**  
The General Partner, Wesley Steehouwer, has exclusive authority to manage investments, execute transactions, and maintain records, acting with the care and diligence of a prudent investment manager. Limited Partners do not participate in management to preserve their limited liability status.

**Lock-Up and Withdrawals**  
Capital contributions are subject to a 7-year lock-up period. Post-lock-up, withdrawals require 12 months’ notice, with a 5% penalty for early withdrawals during the lock-up period (except for cause).

**Annual Meetings and Reporting**  
Partners will gather annually in Thailand to review performance and strategy. The General Partner will provide audited annual financial statements, quarterly account statements, and detailed investment letters every 6-12 months.

<div class="content-section glass-card">
  <h2 class="section-title">Investment Strategy and Restrictions</h2>

  ### Permitted Investments
  The Partnership may invest in:  
  - Public and private equity securities  
  - Debt and fixed income securities  
  - Real estate and infrastructure  
  - Alternative investments (hedge funds, private equity)  
  - Currency and commodity investments  
  - Other opportunities deemed appropriate by the General Partner  

  ### Concentration and Leverage
  - Up to 25% of net assets may be invested in a single security.  
  - Leverage is limited to 200% of net asset value, used prudently to enhance returns.  

  ### Books and Records
  The General Partner maintains a detailed capital ledger tracking contributions, profits, losses, and distributions. Limited Partners have inspection rights during normal business hours, with strict confidentiality obligations.

  ### Transfer Restrictions
  Partnership interests cannot be transferred without General Partner consent, except to immediate family, controlled entities, or existing Limited Partners, subject to securities laws and a right of first refusal.
</div>

<div class="content-section glass-card">
  <h2 class="section-title">Dissolution and Liquidation</h2>

  The Partnership will dissolve upon:  
  - Expiration of the 20-year term  
  - Consent of Partners holding 75% of interests  
  - General Partner’s death, disability, or withdrawal  
  - Conversion to an Irish PLC  
  - Other events under applicable law  

  Upon dissolution, assets will be liquidated, debts paid, capital contributions returned, and remaining proceeds distributed pro rata based on capital account balances.
