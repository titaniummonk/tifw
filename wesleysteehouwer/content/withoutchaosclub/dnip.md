## Partnership Overview

The Digital Nomad Investment Partnership, led by Wesley Steehouwer (The Titanium Monk), is a Scottish General Partnership formed under the Partnership Act 1890 and Limited Partnerships Act 1907. This agreement outlines the structure, terms, and objectives for a disciplined, long-term investment strategy targeting superior capital appreciation through concentrated investments in undervalued securities, with particular expertise in Southeast Asian markets.

## Key Terms and Operations

**Management and Control**  
The General Partner has exclusive authority to manage investments, execute transactions, and maintain records, acting with the care and diligence of a prudent investment manager. Limited Partners do not participate in management to preserve their limited liability status.

**Dynamic Capacity Management**  
The Partnership employs sophisticated capacity management to optimize returns. During superior performance periods (25%+ annual returns for three consecutive years), capacity may expand. During challenging periods (below 5% annual returns for three consecutive years), capacity may be restricted to focus on existing partners.

**Multi-Currency Framework**  
Partners may contribute in GBP, USD, or EUR. All contributions convert to GBP equivalent for accounting purposes, with currency benefits/losses accruing to the Partnership.

<div class="content-section glass-card">
  <h2 class="section-title">Investment Strategy and Operations</h2>

  ### General Partner Commitment
  The General Partner commits not less than 25% of their liquid net worth to the Partnership within 90 days of the first Limited Partner admission, ensuring complete alignment of interests.

  ### Permitted Investments
  The Partnership may invest in:  
  - Public and private equity securities  
  - Debt instruments and fixed income securities  
  - Real estate and infrastructure investments  
  - Alternative investments (hedge funds, private equity)  
  - Currency and commodity investments  
  - Other opportunities deemed appropriate by the General Partner  

  ### Investment Parameters
  - **Concentration**: Up to 25% of net assets in a single security  
  - **Leverage**: Limited to 200% of net asset value, used prudently  
  - **Geographic Focus**: Global opportunities with Southeast Asian expertise  

  ### Capital Ledger System
  The Partnership maintains a comprehensive capital ledger recording each Partner's:
  - Capital contributions by class and date
  - Allocated profits and losses
  - Distributions received
  - Current capital account balance
  - Performance tracking by membership class

  ### Privacy Protection
  To maximize partner privacy:
  - No collective schedule of Partners maintained
  - Individual partner information accessible only to that specific Partner
  - Partner identity information segregated from general Partnership records
  - Each Partner documented solely through individual Joinder Agreements
</div>

<div class="content-section glass-card">
  <h2 class="section-title">Regulatory Compliance and Capacity</h2>

  ### Regulatory Limits
  To ensure securities law compliance:
  - **Delta Class**: Maximum 66 Limited Partners
  - **Gamma Class**: Maximum 33 Limited Partners
  - **Other Classes**: No specific numerical limits
  - **Minimum Partnership Capital**: £100,000 equivalent to commence operations

  ### Performance-Based Capacity Adjustments
  
  **Superior Performance Expansion** (25%+ annual returns for 3 consecutive years):
  - Capacity may increase up to 100% of then-current Partnership assets
  - Additional membership classes may be opened
  - Geographic recruitment may expand
  
  **Exceptional Performance Recognition** (300%+ cumulative returns over 5 years):
  - Capacity restrictions may be eliminated for up to 24 months
  - Special opportunity classes for large transactions
  - Complementary investment vehicles for specialized strategies
  
  **Performance-Challenged Restriction** (Below 5% annual returns for 3 consecutive years):
  - Temporary capacity restrictions to focus on existing Partners
  - New partner admissions may be suspended
  - Administrative complexity may be reduced

  ### Market Condition Adjustments
  - **Bear Markets**: Capacity may expand to capitalize on opportunities
  - **Bull Markets**: Capacity may be restricted to maintain selectivity
  - **Sector Opportunities**: Adjustments for specific investment themes
</div>

<div class="content-section glass-card">
  <h2 class="section-title">Transfer and Dissolution Provisions</h2>

  ### Transfer Restrictions
  Partnership interests cannot be transferred without General Partner consent, except to:
  - Immediate family members or trusts
  - Entities controlled by the Limited Partner
  - Other existing Limited Partners of same or higher class
  - Subject to securities laws and right of first refusal

  ### Dissolution Events
  The Partnership dissolves upon:
  - Expiration of the 30-year term
  - General Partner's discretion
  - General Partner's death, disability, or withdrawal
  - Conversion to PLC structure
  - Other events under applicable law

  ### Liquidation Process
  Upon dissolution:
  1. Payment of Partnership debts and obligations
  2. Return of Partners' capital contributions per capital ledger
  3. Distribution of remaining assets pro rata based on capital account balances
</div>
